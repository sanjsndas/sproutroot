Cookies Policy

Last Updated: January 15, 2023

SproutRoot Care uses cookies and similar technologies to provide and improve our services, enhance your user experience, and analyze website traffic. By using our website, you consent to our use of cookies in accordance with this policy. This Cookies Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and how you can manage your cookie preferences.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device when you visit them. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide features, and collect information about how visitors use sites. Cookies typically don't contain personally identifiable information but may be used in combination with other data to recognize you. There are several types of cookies, including session cookies that expire when you close your browser and persistent cookies that remain until deleted or expired.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for various purposes including essential site functionality, performance analysis, user preferences, and marketing. Essential cookies help our website function properly, allowing you to navigate and use features. Performance cookies collect anonymous data about how visitors use our site, helping us improve its functionality. Preference cookies remember your choices, such as language or region settings. Marketing cookies track browsing habits to deliver targeted advertising relevant to you.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Our website uses first-party cookies (set by our domain) and third-party cookies (set by other domains). First-party cookies are primarily for essential functions and analytics. Third-party cookies include services like Google Analytics for website statistics, social media cookies for sharing content, and advertising cookies for targeted marketing. We classify cookies as strictly necessary (essential), performance (analytics), functionality (preferences), and targeting (advertising) based on their purpose.

4. Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for our website to function properly and cannot be switched off. They are typically set in response to actions you take, such as setting your privacy preferences or filling in forms. Essential cookies enable core functionality like security, network management, and accessibility. Without these cookies, our website may not work correctly, and some features may be unavailable. These cookies don't store personally identifiable information.

5. Performance Cookies

Performance cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. They allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve site performance. These cookies help us know which pages are most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. The information these cookies collect is aggregated and anonymous, not used to identify you personally. We use services like Google Analytics for this purpose.

6. Functionality Cookies

Functionality cookies enable the website to provide enhanced features and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we've added to our pages. These cookies remember choices you make (like your username, language, or region) to provide a more personalized experience. The information these cookies collect may be anonymized and they cannot track your browsing activity on other websites. Without these cookies, certain functionality may become unavailable.

7. Targeting Cookies

Targeting cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners to build a profile of your interests. They work by uniquely identifying your browser and internet device to show you relevant advertisements on other sites. These cookies are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you don't allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising. We use services like Facebook Pixel and Google Ads for these purposes.

8. Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may use various third-party cookies for different purposes. Social media cookies enable you to share content from our site through platforms like Facebook and Twitter. Advertising networks may set cookies to deliver relevant ads to you. Analytics services use cookies to understand how users engage with our site. Some third-party services may combine cookie data with other information they have collected for their own purposes.

9. Cookie Duration

Cookies can remain on your device for different periods of time. Session cookies are temporary and expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain on your device for the period specified in the cookie or until you manually delete them. Some cookies expire after a few hours or days, while others may last for several years. The duration depends on the purpose of the cookie and the technology used to implement it. We regularly review our cookie usage and update durations as necessary.

10. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in various ways through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to view what cookies are stored, delete individual cookies, block cookies from particular sites, or block all cookies. Be aware that if you disable cookies entirely, some website features may not work as intended. Our cookie consent tool allows you to opt in or out of different categories of cookies while maintaining essential functionality. You can change your preferences at any time.

11. Browser-Specific Instructions

For Chrome: Click the three dots > Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data. For Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data. For Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data. For Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Manage and delete cookies and site data. For Internet Explorer: Tools > Internet options > Privacy tab. Each browser has different options for managing cookies, including blocking third-party cookies or deleting cookies when you close the browser.

12. Mobile Browser Management

Managing cookies on mobile browsers follows similar principles to desktop browsers. For iOS Safari: Settings > Safari > Privacy & Security. For Android Chrome: Menu > Settings > Site settings > Cookies. Mobile browsers may offer additional options like blocking third-party cookies by default. Some mobile operating systems also provide system-wide controls for tracking across apps and websites. App ecosystems may have their own cookie-like tracking technologies that require separate management.

13. European Union Compliance

In compliance with EU cookie laws, we provide clear information about cookies and obtain consent for non-essential cookies. Our cookie consent tool allows users to choose which categories of cookies to accept. We maintain records of consent and make it easy for users to change their preferences. The only cookies that don't require consent are those strictly necessary for basic website functionality. Our cookie policy is reviewed regularly to ensure compliance with evolving regulations.

14. California Consumer Privacy Act

Under CCPA regulations, California residents have the right to know what personal information is collected through cookies and opt-out of the sale of such information. While we don't sell personal information in the traditional sense, some targeted advertising may be considered a "sale" under CCPA. California residents can opt out of targeted advertising cookies through our consent management tool. We provide specific disclosures about data collection purposes and sources.

15. GDPR Compliance

Our cookie practices comply with General Data Protection Regulation requirements for lawful processing of personal data. We provide clear information about data processing through cookies and obtain explicit consent where required. Users can withdraw consent as easily as giving it. We conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments for our cookie-related processing activities. Our Data Protection Officer oversees compliance with GDPR cookie requirements.

17. Do Not Track Signals

Our website respects Do Not Track (DNT) browser signals. When we detect a DNT signal, we disable all non-essential cookies and tracking technologies by default. However, some features may not work properly without certain cookies. The DNT standard hasn't been universally adopted, so our response to DNT signals may differ from how other websites respond. We periodically review our DNT implementation as the standard evolves.
